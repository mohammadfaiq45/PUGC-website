Department of Law

LLB (Hons.)

LLB (Hons.) - 5 Year Program

The LLB (Hons.) program is a 5-year course designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the legal system, its frameworks, and its application to real-world scenarios. The curriculum covers diverse areas of law, including constitutional law, criminal law, corporate law, and human rights, ensuring that graduates are equipped with the knowledge and skills necessary to excel as legal professionals.

Program Structure: The LLB (Hons.) program is structured over 10 semesters.

Core Courses: Students will study foundational legal subjects such as:

  • Constitutional Law
  • Criminal Law
  • Corporate Law
  • Human Rights Law
  • Civil Procedure

Practical Exposure: The program includes moot court competitions, legal clinics, and internships to provide hands-on experience.

Eligibility: F.A / A-Level or equivalent with a minimum of 60% marks in the relevant discipline.

Admission Criteria:

  • Basic Additional Marks: Hafiz-e-Quran: 20 Marks
  • Merit Formula: Academic Record: 100%

Department Overview

The Law department offers programs in legal studies, focusing on constitutional law, criminal justice, civil rights, and international law, preparing students for careers in legal practice or public service. The curriculum is designed to build a strong foundation in legal reasoning, research, and advocacy skills, equipping students to navigate complex legal systems and effectively represent clients or causes.

This well-rounded education prepares students for roles in litigation, corporate law, human rights, or government service, and for further specialization through advanced legal studies.
